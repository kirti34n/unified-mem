# Research foundations

The design rules are distilled from published work. **ACE** ([arXiv 2510.04618](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.04618)): evolving playbooks, context-collapse and brevity-bias failure modes, hence incremental note operations and never wholesale rewrites (its reported +10.6% on agent benchmarks is the flagship result behind the design). **MemRL** ([arXiv 2601.03192](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.03192)): two-phase retrieval, a semantic filter then a Q-value re-rank, and a `Q <- Q + a(r - Q)` outcome update; the contribution weight `c` in our variant is our own extension, inspired by TAME's contribution-aware evaluation (TAME itself uses set-union memory and cosine-threshold retrieval, not a Q update). **CODESKILL**: verifiable rewards beat judge-only scoring. **SCM** ([arXiv 2604.20943](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.20943)): sleep-time consolidation with algorithmic forgetting, reporting ~90% memory-noise reduction while keeping recall. (The often-quoted "~30% redundant entries by session ten" figure comes from the [AutoDream blog](https://zenvanriel.com), not a paper, and is treated as motivation, not a research result.) **SleepGate**: stale-retrieval rate as a first-class metric. **A-MEM** ([arXiv 2606.24937](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.24937)): atomic Zettelkasten-style linked notes beat raw chunks. **MemFail** ([arXiv 2605.26667](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.26667)): memory systems fail at the write step through over-compression and bad merges, hence the verbatim rule and the arbiter. **"Context Length Alone Hurts LLM Performance"** ([arXiv 2510.05381](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.05381), Findings of EMNLP 2025): even irrelevant filler degrades performance, hence the aggressive relevance floors and the inject-nothing default. **LongMemEval** ([arXiv 2410.10813](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10813)): abstention ability as a first-class measure, hence negative probes.
